MMD Player

A WebGPU MMD preview tool that verifies motion compatibility with characters and automatically corrects body proportion differences
Tool Design, Shader Development, Motion Analysis Feb–Mar 2026 Personal Project
Three.js WebGPU (r172) TSL Shader Vanilla JavaScript

Motivation

While building a PMX-to-VRM conversion pipeline, I needed a tool to verify that the original model and motion work correctly before conversion. The key challenge was distinguishing whether import failures were caused by the original PMX or the VRM converter — if the PMX itself has missing textures or absent bones, it is not a converter issue.

Target users: planners and QA — not just TAs, but non-developer roles who can open and verify directly.

Solution

Automatic Motion Compatibility Analysis

Analyzes VMD motion files to calculate bone match rates against PMX models, and automatically detects IK conflicts and source model families. Game studio PMX models also follow these families — e.g., Honkai: Star Rail uses the DIVA family, Genshin Impact uses the ミリシタ family.

Family Characteristics
あにまさ式 (Animasa)Default MMD model, minimal bone set (~30)
TDA式Includes semi-standard bones (上半身2, 腕捩, etc.)
YYB式TDA-based + extended auxiliary bones
にがもん式Compact bone set, non-Vocaloid characters
つみだんご式High-density custom bones (325), 50 expressions
ミリシタRomanized bone names, incompatible with standard VMD

Automatic Body Proportion Correction (IK Sizing)

Automatically scales IK positions based on the hip height ratio between models. Even without a source model, the original body proportions are estimated from the median of ground-contact frames in the VMD animation.

Body Proportion Correction Before/After — Comparison before and after IK sizing (floating feet to ground contact)

Motion-synced Effects — Precompute Experiment

An experiment in precomputing CPU-based effects such as vector fields from VMD motion data. Results were excellent — lightweight, and effect timing is automatically derived from motion tracks alone. A POC to demonstrate to planning and client teams that "it moves well."

6 types: Foot Ripple, Spark Burst, Ground Reflect, Falling/Rising Light, Velocity Effect

Technical Details

VMD Precompute — Effect Pre-calculation
Pre-calculates bloom events, foot landing timings, and angular velocity peaks from motion data. Synchronizes effects without frame drops by using precompute instead of real-time analysis.
IK Sizing — Body Estimation Without Source Model
Detects ground contact frames based on IK Y position from the target model's rest pose. Estimates the source model's hip height from the median center Y of those frames, then scales IK/center/groove Y positions by the targetHipY / sourceHipY ratio.
TSL Shaders — WebGPU Custom Materials
Custom materials based on Three.js TSL (Three Shading Language). Ground Mirror Reflection, effect particles, and more implemented as TSL nodes. Hands-on validation of WebGPU's next-gen rendering pipeline.
VMD Binary Parser + Compatibility Analysis
Parses bone keyframes, morph keyframes, and IK keyframes from VMD files. Compares against the PMX model's bone tree to automatically report match rates, missing bones, and IK conflicts.
